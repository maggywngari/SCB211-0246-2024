Chocolate Banana Bread

This Chocolate Banana Bread is my go-to comfort dessert. It combines the sweetness of ripe bananas with rich chocolate for a treat that's both indulgent and nostalgic. The recipe is simple enough for beginners but the results are impressive enough to serve to guests.

Prep: 15 mins
Cook: 60 mins
Serves: 10 slices
Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 3 very ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 3/4 cup (150g) brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (50g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup (175g) chocolate chips, divided
  • 1/2 cup (60g) chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line a 9×5-inch loaf pan with parchment paper.

  2. Mix Wet Ingredients

    In a large bowl, combine the mashed bananas with the melted butter. Stir in the brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  3. Combine Dry Ingredients

    In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.

  4. Combine Mixtures

    Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring just until combined. Do not overmix.

  5. Add Mix-ins

    Fold in 3/4 cup of the chocolate chips and the walnuts (if using), reserving the remaining chocolate chips for the top.

  6. Transfer to Pan

    Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan. Sprinkle the remaining 1/4 cup chocolate chips on top.

  7. Bake

    Bake for 55-65 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter).

  8. Cool and Serve

    Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Nutritional Information (Per Slice)

Calories: 320
Protein: 5g
Carbohydrates: 45g
Fat: 15g
Sugar: 25g
Fiber: 3g
