Kenyan Pilau

Kenyan Pilau is a flavorful rice dish that holds a special place in my heart. It brings together aromatic spices and tender meat for a comforting meal that's perfect for gatherings. This dish is a staple in Kenyan cuisine and is often served during celebrations and special occasions.

Prep: 20 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serves: 6
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g beef or goat meat, cut into cubes
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons pilau masala (or mix of cumin, coriander, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and quartered (optional)
  • 4 cups beef or chicken stock
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat

    In a large pot, boil the meat with a pinch of salt until tender, about 20-30 minutes. Reserve the broth.

  2. Sauté the Base

    In a separate large pot, heat oil and sauté onions until golden brown. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Add Spices

    Add the pilau masala and stir for 30 seconds until aromatic.

  4. Combine Ingredients

    Add the cooked meat, diced tomatoes, and potatoes (if using). Stir to coat with the spices.

  5. Cook the Rice

    Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir gently to mix with other ingredients.

  6. Add Liquid

    Pour in the reserved meat broth and additional stock to make 4 cups of liquid. Add salt to taste.

  7. Simmer

    Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.

  8. Rest and Serve

    Remove from heat and let it rest, covered, for 10 minutes. Fluff with a fork, garnish with fresh coriander, and serve.

Nutritional Information (Per Serving)

Calories: 450
Protein: 25g
Carbohydrates: 55g
Fat: 15g
Fiber: 3g
Sodium: 480mg
